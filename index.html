    Why Africa?

    For a considerable portion of human history, Africa has been overlooked in terms of high quality data, predictions, and information. Given the many rapidly emerging economies in the continent of Africa over the last decade which have the power to shape the global economic landscape as we move forwards and look towards the rest of the 21st century, it is imperative that we close the gap of high quality, accurate population data and predictions for the continent, which has the potential to guide policymakers, both national and international, in making key decisions of critical nature to the well-being of the continent or individual sub-countries, including the equitable allocation and distribution of government financial or economic resources that will drive the continent of country’s growth over the near future.
    In keeping this background in mind, it is interesting to highlight that given the stark education divide between urban and rural communities even within the same country or jurisdiction, Nairobi’s teenage crisis, as well as shifting industry dynamics and economic changes across different counties over time, we see Africa as an important geographic region worth analyzing closely over the near future as the many emerging economies within this region have the power to shape and influence the global economy.

    Why Kenya?

    Given Kenya’s rapidly growing population over the last decade, one big area of opportunity worth looking into for Microsoft is an in-depth understanding of the key growth sectors in Kenya. Our data-driven tool powers key analyses that can guide key policymakers both domestically (e.g. national government officials) and internationally (e.g. cross-border international organization such as the United Nations) in making integral decisions on the allocation of both financial and economic resources to more promising cities that we expect to be of high growth potential into the future.

    Integrity of Historical Data

    Given the very limited sources of accurate data currently available for Kenya (e.g. Garissa’s census crisis, high fluctuation of gross county product (GCP) (defined as GDP in county-level terms) in numerous counties within Kenya, etc…), our team has decided to focus on bridging the gap to provide representation data projections and predictions by building a tool that allows users to incorporate his or her own expectations of a country and/or locality into the projection model. With this additional flexibility, users are able to understand the projected future economic growth on the county-level in Kenya at a deeper level while factoring in their own expectations, which allows key policymakers to plan for potential investments in key counties in the near future in a continent that’s been a critical focus for global growth.

    One key innovative feature of our tool is the incorporation of additional functionality to provide risk analyses, which allows users to interpolate from the tool’s results and predictions, export these results, and extract key pieces of information, insight, and recommendations to be able to flexibly adapt the results to their needs and draw insights that are aligned with the various different goals of their analyses. Key applications of our tool include identifying which age groups of the population in Kenya contribute and/or matter the most to GCP and/or GDP growth in the near future, which allows users to extract interesting information and insights derived from our tool that would benefit from further exploration to better understand the future of the socioeconomic status of Kenya on a more granular level to inform both current and future decision-making of all granularities and scales.
